Official Overview

What is CEnT-S

CEnT-S is the new admission test managed by CISIA for students applying to English-taught degree programmes in Italy. Starting November 2025, CEnT-S replaces previous English TOLC-E, TOLC-I, and TOLC-F formats.

Engineering Economics Pharmacy Sciences
Always check the admission notice from your chosen university for the latest CEnT-S requirements.

Programs It’s For

CEnT-S is used by selected degree programmes mainly taught in English. It is especially relevant for students applying to programmes in engineering, economics, pharmacy, and related scientific fields.

Bachelor Degrees English-Taught Italy Scientific Areas
Universities may use the result differently, so always verify the programme-specific admission rules.

What It Replaced

CEnT-S replaces the previous English CISIA formats that students used for similar admission pathways. The new unified format is designed to streamline access to selected English-taught programmes.

TOLC-E TOLC-I TOLC-F
The updated structure makes the process clearer, but students should still check what their target university accepts.

Check Your University’s Rules

Every university and programme may define its own way of using the CEnT-S result in the admission process. Requirements, accepted formats, deadlines, and selection criteria can differ.

Before booking a CEnT-S, students should always read the official notice of the programme they are applying to.
Exam Structure

Sections, timing, and
what each part tests

CEnT-S is divided into 5 timed sections. Each part evaluates a different academic skill needed for English-taught university programmes.

Official Format

CEnT-S Overview

55 Questions
110 Minutes
  • Mathematics 15 / 30 min
  • Reasoning on texts and data 15 / 30 min
  • Biology 10 / 20 min
  • Chemistry 10 / 20 min
  • Physics 5 / 10 min

Mathematics

This section evaluates core mathematical readiness for university study. It includes algebra, geometry, functions, graphs, percentages, statistics, and practical problem solving.

Reasoning on texts and data

This section focuses on logic, understanding written information, interpreting data, and drawing correct conclusions from structured material.

Biology

Biology checks basic scientific knowledge relevant to selected programmes and helps assess the student’s readiness for academic study in science-related fields.

Chemistry

Chemistry tests essential concepts and the ability to interpret scientific information clearly and accurately.

Physics

Physics is the shortest section but still important for the overall result. It checks scientific reasoning and the ability to solve problems efficiently.
